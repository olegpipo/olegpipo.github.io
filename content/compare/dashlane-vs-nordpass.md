Dashlane and NordPass sit at opposite ends of the password manager pricing spectrum, and the gap between them raises an obvious question: is Dashlane's $59.99/year premium experience worth $36 more per year than NordPass's streamlined $23.88/year alternative? This comparison is part of our [password manager comparisons hub](/compare/), where we evaluate every major option so you can decide based on facts rather than marketing.

Both products have clean security records and solid encryption. Both handle the daily essentials of password management -- storing credentials, generating passwords, autofilling forms. The difference lies in what they bundle beyond the basics and how they price it. Dashlane wraps in a VPN, dark web monitoring, and phishing alerts. NordPass keeps things leaner and passes the savings to you. Let's dig into whether the extras justify the price.

## Dashlane: The All-Inclusive Premium

Dashlane has been in the password management space since 2012, and it has consistently positioned itself as the premium option. The product is polished, feature-rich, and expensive. At $59.99/year for Premium, it costs more than most competitors -- and Dashlane's argument is that you get more than most competitors offer.

The standout extras that justify the price (in Dashlane's view):

**Built-in VPN.** Dashlane is the only major password manager that includes a VPN, powered by Hotspot Shield. It provides basic network privacy on public Wi-Fi -- not a replacement for a dedicated VPN service, but a useful addition for users who do not already have one. A standalone VPN typically costs $50-100/year, so this bundled feature has real monetary value.

**Dark web monitoring.** Dashlane continuously scans breach databases for your registered email addresses and alerts you when associated credentials appear in known data leaks. This is proactive monitoring, not just checking your existing passwords against a database.

**Phishing alerts.** Dashlane warns you when you visit suspicious sites that may be attempting to steal credentials. This goes beyond password management into broader security protection.

**Password health dashboard.** A visual score of your overall password security posture, with actionable recommendations for fixing weak, reused, or compromised credentials.

The interface is Dashlane's other competitive advantage. The company invests heavily in design, and the vault experience is clean, visually appealing, and guided. Onboarding walks new users through each step, and the overall experience is designed for people who want security without complexity.

Dashlane uses AES-256 encryption with Argon2d key derivation and zero-knowledge architecture. No desktop app -- it operates through browser extensions and a web vault on desktop. The free tier offers 25 passwords on a single device, which is barely functional.

## NordPass: Streamlined and Affordable

NordPass launched in 2019 as part of the Nord Security ecosystem, which also includes NordVPN, NordLayer, and NordLocker. It is the youngest major password manager in this comparison, but it backed by a company with deep experience in online security infrastructure.

NordPass's most distinctive technical feature is its encryption choice. While most password managers use AES-256, NordPass uses **XChaCha20**, a modern stream cipher that is a variant of the ChaCha20 algorithm. XChaCha20 is considered equally secure to AES-256 by cryptography experts, but it has some practical advantages: it performs better on devices without hardware AES acceleration, and its nonce-misuse resistance provides an additional safety margin against implementation errors.

At $23.88/year for the Premium plan, NordPass costs less than half of what Dashlane charges. Despite the lower price, the feature set covers the essentials well:

- Unlimited passwords across unlimited devices
- Password generator with customizable rules
- AutoFill across browsers and apps
- Secure notes, credit cards, and personal information storage
- Passkey support
- Password health monitoring
- **Data Breach Scanner** -- checks your email addresses and passwords against known breach databases
- **Email Masking** -- hides your real email address when signing up for new services
- Biometric unlock (Face ID, Touch ID, fingerprint, Windows Hello)

NordPass Premium also includes a password sharing feature and the ability to grant emergency access to trusted contacts. The interface is clean and modern, reflecting the design sensibility of the broader Nord ecosystem.

The free tier is more generous than Dashlane's: unlimited passwords but limited to one device at a time (you must log out of one device to access on another). It is usable for a single-device workflow but frustrating for anyone who switches between phone and computer regularly.

## Security Architecture

Both Dashlane and NordPass maintain clean security records with no known breaches of customer vault data.

### Dashlane Security

- **Encryption**: AES-256-bit with Argon2d key derivation
- **Architecture**: Zero-knowledge; Dashlane cannot access vault data
- **Source code**: Proprietary, closed source
- **Audits**: Independent security audits conducted periodically
- **Breach history**: No known breach of customer vault data
- **Infrastructure**: AWS cloud hosting

### NordPass Security

- **Encryption**: XChaCha20 with Argon2id key derivation
- **Architecture**: Zero-knowledge; NordPass cannot access vault data
- **Source code**: Proprietary; security audit results published
- **Audits**: Independent audit by Cure53 (published); SOC 2 Type 2 certification
- **Breach history**: No known breach of customer vault data
- **Infrastructure**: Nord Security cloud infrastructure

### Security Verdict

Both use strong, modern encryption and key derivation. The technical differences are interesting for cryptography enthusiasts but practically equivalent for most users:

- Dashlane uses **AES-256 + Argon2d**. AES-256 is the industry standard, battle-tested and widely trusted. Argon2d is optimized for side-channel resistance.
- NordPass uses **XChaCha20 + Argon2id**. XChaCha20 is a modern stream cipher that performs well in software and has strong nonce-misuse resistance. Argon2id combines resistance to both side-channel attacks and GPU-based brute-force attacks.

Both approaches are considered secure by the cryptographic community. NordPass has a slight edge in transparency: its Cure53 audit results are publicly available, and it holds SOC 2 Type 2 certification. Dashlane's audit results are shared more selectively.

Neither has been breached. Both are trustworthy.

### Where Dashlane Leads

**Built-in VPN.** No other password manager bundles a VPN. If you do not have a VPN and want basic network privacy included with your password manager, Dashlane is the only option that delivers this. The VPN alone can justify a significant portion of the price difference for users who would otherwise purchase one separately.

**TOTP authenticator.** Dashlane stores and auto-fills TOTP two-factor authentication codes directly within the vault. NordPass does not include a built-in TOTP authenticator -- it offers NordPass Authenticator as a separate app. For users who want their 2FA codes alongside their passwords in one interface, Dashlane is more convenient.

**Phishing alerts.** Dashlane warns you about suspicious websites that may be phishing attempts. NordPass does not offer this protection. For less security-savvy users, this is a meaningful safety layer.

**Encrypted file storage.** Dashlane Premium includes 1 GB of encrypted file storage for sensitive documents. NordPass does not offer encrypted file storage at any tier. If you need to store scans of IDs, insurance cards, or other documents alongside your passwords, Dashlane has this covered.

**Richer dark web monitoring.** Both products scan for breached credentials, but Dashlane's dark web monitoring is more comprehensive, continuously scanning multiple sources beyond just known breach databases.

**Password changer.** Dashlane can automatically change passwords on certain supported websites. The feature is limited in scope but time-saving when it works.

### Where NordPass Leads

**Price.** This is NordPass's most significant advantage. At $23.88/year, it costs less than half of Dashlane's $59.99/year. For users who need solid password management without premium extras, NordPass delivers substantial savings.

**Email Masking.** NordPass Premium includes email masking, which lets you create unique email addresses when signing up for services. This keeps your real email address private and reduces spam. Dashlane does not offer this feature.

**Desktop apps.** NordPass provides native desktop applications for Windows, macOS, and Linux. Dashlane discontinued its desktop apps in 2022 and operates through browser extensions and the web vault only. NordPass's desktop app provides vault access without needing a browser open and supports offline access to cached credentials.

**XChaCha20 encryption.** While both encryption approaches are considered secure, XChaCha20 is a newer algorithm with strong theoretical properties, including better nonce-misuse resistance. For users who prefer modern cryptographic choices, NordPass's approach is appealing.

**Better free tier.** NordPass Free offers unlimited passwords (though only on one device at a time). Dashlane Free caps you at 25 passwords on one device. NordPass Free is more usable as a standalone password manager, even with the single-device-at-a-time limitation.

**Nord ecosystem integration.** For users already in the Nord Security ecosystem (NordVPN, NordLocker, NordLayer), NordPass integrates naturally. Bundle pricing through Nord's combined plans can reduce the per-product cost further.

**Browser support.** NordPass supports Brave browser natively, in addition to the standard Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge, and Opera extensions. Dashlane does not offer a Brave extension.

## Consider Also: A Different Approach

Both Dashlane and NordPass are subscription-based password managers that store your encrypted vault on company servers. They are different products from different companies, but the model is the same: you pay annually, your data lives in their cloud, and if you stop paying, you lose access to premium features.

**PanicVault** offers an alternative for Apple users who want to step off the subscription treadmill entirely. It is a KeePass-compatible password manager built natively for Apple devices. Your vault is a standard KDBX file -- an open format that works with dozens of apps on every platform.

- **One-time purchase** -- no subscription, no renewal, no price increases. Pay once and own it.
- **Open KDBX format** -- your data is portable and never locked to any vendor
- **TOTP codes built in** -- no separate authenticator app, no premium tier
- **iCloud and Google Drive sync** -- your encrypted file syncs through services you already trust
- **Apple-native design** -- Face ID, Touch ID, AutoFill, widgets, Shortcuts integration
- **Offline-first** -- works without internet; your vault is a local file

PanicVault does not include a VPN, dark web monitoring, or email masking. What it offers is the end of recurring costs and complete ownership of both your password manager and your data. Over five years, Dashlane costs $299.95 and NordPass costs $119.40. PanicVault costs its one-time purchase price -- once -- and there is nothing more to pay.

## The Bottom Line

NordPass is the better value for most users making this comparison. It covers the core password management essentials -- unlimited passwords, cross-device sync, password generation, autofill, breach scanning -- at $23.88/year. That is less than half of Dashlane's price, and for the majority of users, the core features are what matter.

Dashlane earns its premium if you will actually use the extras. The bundled VPN has real value for users who need one. Dark web monitoring and phishing alerts add security layers that NordPass does not match. The TOTP authenticator is more convenient than NordPass's separate app. If you will use all of these features, Dashlane's $59.99/year can be justified as a bundle deal.

But if you are honest about what you use daily -- and for most people, that is storing passwords, generating new ones, and having them autofill -- NordPass does the job well for $36 less per year. That $180 in savings over five years buys a lot of password management for money that stays in your pocket.
