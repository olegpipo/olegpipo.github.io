Pinterest may seem like a low-stakes platform compared to Facebook or Google, but it collects more data than most users realize. Pinterest tracks your search behavior, the pins you view and save, your browsing activity on websites with Pinterest tracking pixels, and builds detailed interest profiles used for advertising. If you have decided that the privacy trade-off is no longer worth the inspiration boards, this guide walks you through deleting your Pinterest account as part of your broader [Digital Privacy & Online Safety guide](/digital-privacy/).

Pinterest is notable for offering both deactivation and deletion as distinct options, and for having a shorter grace period than most platforms -- just 14 days compared to the 30-day window most social networks provide. Make sure you have saved everything you want before initiating the process.

## Before You Delete: Back Up Your Data

Pinterest boards can represent years of curated inspiration, project planning, and saved ideas. Take time to preserve what matters.

### Download Your Pinterest Data

1. Log in to **pinterest.com** on a desktop browser
2. Click your **profile picture** (top right)
3. Click **Settings** (gear icon)
4. Click **Privacy and Data** in the left sidebar
5. Scroll to **Request Your Data**
6. Click **Request Data**
7. Pinterest will email you a download link when your data is ready

The data export includes your profile information, boards, pins, comments, messages, search history, and settings.

### Save Your Boards and Pins

The data export may not include full-resolution images for all your pins. To save your boards more thoroughly:

**Individual pins:**
1. Open each pin you want to save
2. Click the pin image to view it at full size
3. Right-click and save the image to your computer
4. Note the source URL if you want to revisit the original content

**Board organization:**
- Take screenshots of your board layouts if the visual organization matters to you
- Copy the source URLs for pins that link to recipes, tutorials, or articles you want to revisit later
- Consider using a bookmarking service like Raindrop.io or browser bookmarks as an alternative to Pinterest's organizational system

### What to Check Before Deleting

- **Boards with collaborators** -- If you have group boards, removing your account will remove you as a contributor. Pins you added to group boards may be removed as well. Notify collaborators before deleting.
- **Business account features** -- If you have a Pinterest Business account with ads, promoted pins, or analytics data, export your analytics and resolve any pending billing before deletion.
- **Connected website** -- If you verified your website with Pinterest and use Rich Pins, those features will stop working after deletion.
- **Third-party logins** -- If you use "Sign in with Pinterest" on any services, create standalone accounts for those services.
- **Pinterest API integrations** -- If you built or use apps that integrate with the Pinterest API through your account, those integrations will stop functioning.
- **Shopping lists** -- If you use Pinterest's shopping features, save any product lists or wish lists separately.

## Step-by-Step: Delete Pinterest on Desktop

1. Go to **pinterest.com** and log in
2. Click your **profile picture** in the top right
3. Click **Settings** (gear icon)
4. Scroll down to **Account Management** (at the bottom of the settings page)
5. Click **Delete Account**
6. Pinterest will ask why you are leaving (this is optional but presented as a form)
7. Enter your **password** to confirm
8. Click **Delete Account**

Your account enters the 14-day deactivation period immediately. Pinterest will send a confirmation email.

## Step-by-Step: Delete Pinterest on Mobile

### iPhone or Android

1. Open the **Pinterest app**
2. Tap your **profile icon** (bottom right)
3. Tap the **settings gear** (top right, or tap the three dots)
4. Scroll to **Account Management**
5. Tap **Delete Account**
6. Review the information about what deletion means
7. Enter your **password**
8. Tap **Delete Account**

The mobile deletion process triggers the same 14-day deactivation period as the desktop process.

## What Happens to Your Data After Deletion

Pinterest uses a shorter timeline than most platforms:

- **Immediately**: Your profile, boards, and pins become hidden from other Pinterest users. Your account does not appear in search results.
- **Days 1-14 (Deactivation Period)**: Your account is deactivated but recoverable. Log in at any point during this window to cancel the deletion and restore your account.
- **After 14 days**: Pinterest begins permanent deletion. Your account, boards, pins, and associated data are removed permanently and cannot be recovered.

**What is permanently deleted:**
- Your profile, boards, and all pins you created or saved
- Secret boards and their contents
- Direct messages
- Search history and activity data
- Advertising preferences and interest profiles
- Shopping activity and product pins

**Data that may persist:**
- Pins of your original content that other users have saved to their own boards remain on those users' accounts
- Comments you left on other users' pins may remain visible
- Pinterest may retain some data for legal compliance, fraud prevention, and platform safety
- Search engine caches may show your Pinterest profile until they refresh

## Deactivation vs. Deletion

Pinterest offers both options as distinct choices:

| Feature | Deactivation | Deletion |
|---------|-------------|----------|
| Profile hidden | Yes | Yes |
| Data preserved | Yes | No (after 14 days) |
| Reactivation | Log in anytime | Only within 14 days |
| Boards and pins | Preserved | Permanently deleted |
| Followers | Preserved | Lost |
| Recovery possible | Always | Only within 14-day window |

### How to Deactivate Instead of Delete

If you want a break without permanent consequences:

1. Go to **Settings > Account Management**
2. Click **Deactivate Account** (instead of Delete Account)
3. Confirm with your password

Deactivation hides your profile and content but preserves everything. You can reactivate at any time simply by logging in. There is no time limit on deactivation -- your account can remain deactivated indefinitely.

## Alternatives to Deletion

If you are not ready for permanent departure, consider these approaches:

- **Deactivate instead** -- Hide your profile and content without losing them. Reactivate whenever you choose.
- **Make boards secret** -- Convert your boards to secret boards so only you can see them. This keeps your content accessible to you but invisible to others.
- **Limit data collection** -- In Settings > Privacy and Data, disable personalized ads and adjust what data Pinterest collects about your activity.
- **Remove the app** -- Delete the Pinterest app from your phone to reduce casual browsing time while keeping your account intact.
- **Disable browser extension** -- If you have the Pinterest browser extension installed, remove it to stop Pinterest from tracking your browsing activity on other websites.
- **Unlink social accounts** -- Disconnect any linked Facebook, Google, or other social accounts under Settings > Social Permissions.
- **Opt out of search engine indexing** -- In Settings > Privacy, disable the option that allows search engines to index your Pinterest profile.
